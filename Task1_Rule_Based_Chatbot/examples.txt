# Rule-Based Chatbot - Conversation Examples

## Example 1: Greeting Conversation
User: Hello
Bot: Hi there! What can I do for you?

User: How are you?
Bot: I'm doing great, thank you for asking!

User: What is your name?
Bot: I'm a rule-based chatbot created for the CODSOFT AI internship.

User: bye
Bot: Goodbye! Have a great day!

## Example 2: Help Request
User: hi
Bot: Hello! How can I help you today?

User: what can you do?
Bot: I can have basic conversations, answer simple questions, and provide information.

User: help me
Bot: I can help with greetings, basic queries, and general conversation.

User: thanks
Bot: You're welcome!

## Example 3: Fallback Handling
User: hello
Bot: Hey! How are you doing?

User: what is the meaning of life?
Bot: I'm not sure how to respond to that. Can you try rephrasing?

User: tell me about quantum physics
Bot: I don't understand. Could you ask something else?

User: bye
Bot: See you later! Take care!

## Example 4: Mixed Conversation
User: hey
Bot: Hello! How can I help you today?

User: how old are you?
Bot: I'm a program, so I don't have an age in the traditional sense!

User: what time is it?
Bot: I don't have access to real-time time data. Please check your device's clock.

User: thank you
Bot: Happy to help!

User: exit
Bot: Bye! It was nice talking to you!

## Supported Patterns:
- Greetings: hi, hello, hey, good morning/afternoon/evening
- Well-being: how are you, how do you do
- Identity: what is your name, who are you
- Capabilities: what can you do, help me, capabilities
- Farewell: bye, goodbye, see you, exit, quit
- Gratitude: thank you, thanks, thx
- Weather: weather, temperature
- Time: time, current time
- Age: age, how old are you

## Features:
- Case-insensitive pattern matching
- Random response selection for variety
- Fallback responses for unknown inputs
- Graceful exit handling
- Error handling for unexpected inputs
